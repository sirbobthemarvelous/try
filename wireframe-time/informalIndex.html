Nouns have 6 cases: Nominative case: names the subject of the sentence (whatever the statement or question is about) THE(thing) (verb) Vocative case: direct address: Look, (name)! Genitive Case: enable a noun to qualify another noun aka in english: the gardens (of caesar); part (of the army); love (of life); man (of distinction), caesar’s gardens Dative case: Express the object indirectly affected by verb or adjective (to or for in english): I gave a book (to him). He did it (for you). She is unfriendly (to me). This is unsuitable (for warfare) Accusative case: limits the action of the verb: She went (home), I ran a (mile). He saw a (bird). I stayed three (days) by clarifying specifics you limit the action Ablative case: 3 uses Expresses separation (from): He comes (from New York.) She fainted (from hunger). This book was written (by/comes from Dickens) Expresses Location(in, on, at): at seven o’clock; on Tuesday; in Italy Expresses instrument by which/circumstance under which, an action takes place (with): She jumped (with joy). We dig (with shovels.) He came (with his father).
